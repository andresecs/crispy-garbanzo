    Staff Availability System

    Overview

    A Note About the Staff Availability System

    This system is primarily composed of a SharePoint list named Staff Availability. However, the details in this section show the entire system (which includes the use of the Employee Records System and a SharePoint Site).

    The Staff Availability Tool is our in-house system for collecting and managing employee availability throughout the semester. Because our store operates in a university setting, we often see frequent changes in staff schedules, especially with student employees. This tool is designed to work seamlessly with our Microsoft ecosystem to make the availability process easier for both staff and supervisors.

    Using this system helps us:

    • Collect staff availabilities quickly and consistently each semester (or for any period of time).
    • Let supervisors easily review, accept, return, or edit submissions.
    • Automate notifications, tracking, and updates to the Employee Records System.

      Availability Portal

    • For All Staff

    Using the System

    Video Tutorial

    Supervisors

    Requesting Availabilities

    Need to send staff a form to fill out their availability? Read below!

    Requirements:

    All of the following requirements must be met to request avalability:

    • The employee is in the Employee Records System.
    • The employee is not already in the Availability database.
    For a limited time window:

    This section to share with students before start of semester.

    Ideal for collecting responses within a specific timeframe. For example, when students are returning to campus and you need their submissions by a set deadline.

    1. Open the Staff Availability Database
    2. Go to forms (top menu bar).
    3. Open the Availability form.
    4. On the right side, Customize > Settings > Accept Responses
    5. Enter the submission window using the Start Date and End Date. Changes get saved automatically.
    6. Go to Send form.
    7. Copy the link and share it with others.
    For anytime submissions:

    Ideal for collecting responses within any timeframe.

    1. Send Availability Form1 to the employee.

    View, Return, Edit, Confirm, or Delete Availabilities

    Viewing A Submitted Availability
    1. Navigate to the Availability Portal.
    2. Find the employee under the Active Availabilities list.
    3. Scroll sideaways across the table to find any specific detail from the employee.
    Can't find the employee?

    This means they do not have a current submission of an availability.

    Returning A Submitted Availability

    Returning an availability deactivates their current submission and sends them a new Availability form (1).

    1. It sends them the Anytime submissions form.

    If you return a submission, we highly recommend you speak to the employee beforehand to provide some additional context. If the changes are minor, consider modifying the submission yourself. Returning a submission will delete the current record.

    Steps to return:

    1. Navigate to the Availability Portal.
    2. Find the employee under the Active Availabilities list.
    3. Click on the blue, left-pointing arrow next to their name.
    4. Done
    What happens next?
    • The Employee Status and Supervisor Status columns change to Draft and Inactive.
    • The employee receives an email (you will be cc'd in it) requesting that the employee go resubmit their availability. (1)
      • The employee will be given the details of the previous submission to make resubmission easier.
    1. The employee will receive a link to resubmit their availability (this form does not have an expiration date set).

    A few more things:

    When should I return an submission?

    It varies. If the employee asks to make major adustments to their schedule or if you are unhappy with the current submission. Returning it to the employee will change the record's status to "Returned," and the Employee Status to Draft mode.

    Note about returning submissions:

    When you return a submission, the employee receives a personalized link to update their availability. This link remains active even after the original submission window has closed, as long as their record is still in the system. This means they can continue making changes unless you manually restrict access.

    For instance: 1. You return a submission to Jane Doe. 1. Jane Doe receives an email and makes changes. 1. You confirm the changes.

    Jane can still go in to her personalized link afterwards and make more changes.

    The only way around this is for the supervisor to modify the details themselves, or to delete the employee and resend the request for availability.

    Confirming A Submitted/Returned Availability

    Once an employee has successfully submitted a new or returned form, a yellow dot will appear next to their submission. This indicates that the submission is new and has not been reviewed by a supervisor yet.

    Steps to confirm:

    1. Navigate to the Availability Portal.
    2. Find the employee under the Active Availabilities list.
    3. Click on the yellow, dot next to their name.
    4. Done

    Don't see the button? This means the employee has had their availability confirmed already.

    What happens next?
    • The Supervisor Status column changes to Active.
    • The employee receives an email (you will be cc'd in it) confirming that the employee's were successfully received.

    A few more things:

    Need to return the submission again?

    For whatever reason it may be, you may need to request the employee go back again and make changes. You can return a submission by simply clicking on the blue, left arrow. Find our more here.

    Modifying An Availability

    There may be times when returning the entire submission isn't really necessary. Or that you can and want to make the small changes yourself without having to bother the employee with email notifications.

    Steps to modify:

    1. Navigate to the Availability Portal.
    2. Find the employee under the Active Availabilities list.
    3. Click on the gray, pencil next to their name.
    4. This will open the form as a pop-up. Make any changes you'd like.

    A few more things:

    When should I modify instead of returning an submission?

    It depends. If the employee mentions a small mistake directly to you, or if you notice a minor typo or incorrect detail, it may be easier to make the change yourself. Ultimately, it’s up to your discretion.

    However, avoid modifying an employee’s availability without their consent or knowledge — they won’t receive a notification when changes are made on their behalf.

    Removing Availability(ies)

    There may be times when you need to individually remove an employee's availability or multiple availabilities at once.

    Steps to delete:

    1. Navigate to the Availability Portal.
    2. Find the employee under the Active Availabilities list.
    3. Click on the red trash icon next to their name.
    4. This will delete the submission.
    I clicked it but the submission is still there...

    This is normal. It might take a couple minutes to disappear. As long as the red trash icon has disappeared, the submission will be deleted.

    Is clicking the delete button the same as removing an availability?

    No! If you see a button that says "Delete" please do not press it. It does delete their record, but it does not update the Employee Records System to change Availability details of the employee (including the Availability Submitted button, which will then affect the view of Missing Availabilities).

    A few more things:

    Is there a way to mass remove all availabilities?

    Unfortunatel, there is not. You will have to click each entry.

    However, avoid modifying an employee’s availability without their consent or knowledge — they won’t receive a notification when changes are made on their behalf.

    Missing Availabilities (View and/or Request)

    Are you missing someone's availability and don't see it in the spreadsheet? Now you can keep track of missing availabilities too.

    Requirements:

    All of the following requirements must be met to view/request missing avalability:

    • The employee has to be in the Employee Records System.
    • In the ERS, Availability Submitted is marked as No.

    Steps to view who is missing availability:

    1. Navigate to the Availability Portal.
    2. Find the employee under the Missing Availabilities list.

    Reminding availability submission: If you want to send a friendly nudge to fill out their availability, you can click on the bell icon (1).

    1. Please note, you can only send one per day.
    I don't see the employee in the Missing list, and their submission is not in Active...

    The Missing list is filtered to show employees who meet all the following conditions in the Employee Records System:

    • Availability Submitted No
    • Employee Status Active
    • Department is not empty.

    If you do not see an employee, it is because one or more of these conditions is not being met.

    I see an employee both in Active and Missing, which one is true?

    If they are in both, the Active list is true. There must have been a glitch in the automation that changes employee's Availability Submitted column to No. Please report this.

    However, avoid modifying an employee’s availability without their consent or knowledge — they won’t receive a notification when changes are made on their behalf.

    There are employees in here that I won't need availability from ever. How do I remove them?

    You'll need to check them off manually from the Employee Records System. Contact the manager of the Employee Records System and ask them to check off the Availability Submitted field.

    All Staff

    Submitting Availability

    To submit your availability, you must have received an Availability form. (1)

    1. Don't have the form? Please ask a supervisor for the availability form. If they are unsure, redirect them to this site for information.

    Filling out the form:

    1. Employee: Start with your name. If you do not see your name in this list, it means you have not been added to our Employee Records System. Please contact your supervisor. If you get a duplicate error message, it means you already have an entry in our database. Please let your supervisor know you'd like to remove your entry.
    2. Effective Date: Enter the earliest date when this availability can start.
    3. Day Availability: For each day, enter your availability.
    4. Comments: Enter any comments regarding your availability in the box.
    5. Click Submit

    You will then receive an email confirmation with a copy of your submission details.

    Modifying/Deleting an Existing Availability

    Did your availability change? Did you make a mistake on the submission?

    If your availability completely changed:

    1. Reach out to your supervisor and ask them to return your entry.

      If they are unsure how to, redirect them to this site for information.

    2. Once this occurs, you will receive an email prompting you to fill out your availability.

    3. Fill out your new availability

    If your availability slightly changed: (1)

    1. Sometimes it is easier for them to make the adjustments without redoing the entire process.

    Steps:

    1. Reach out to your supervisor and ask them to modify your entry.

      If they are unsure how to, redirect them to this site for information.

    2. Provide the details to modify.

    3. Check for confirmation from your supervisor that the details have been updated.

    The submission confirmation email can help make changes easier:

    In the email, there is a Make changes to my availability and Cancel my availability links. Clicking them creates a pre-drafted email to your supervisor. Add your specific changes and send!

    Other Effects

    Staff Availability

    Whenever staff create new availability for themselves, they look up their name using a dropdown. The dropdown values are pulled directly from the Employee Record System. Therefore, if the employee is not registered in the ERS, they will not be able to add their availability.

    Radio Assignments

    Whenever radios get assigned to staff, the Radio Database uses an Employee Name dropdown to link the employee to the radio. The dropdown values from Employee Name are pulled directly from the Employee Record System. Therefore, if the employee is not registered in the ERS, they will not be found when assigning a radio.

    Views

    In SharePoint Lists, views are customized ways of displaying list data — they let you filter, sort, or group items to focus on specific information. Each view can show different columns, layouts, or criteria depending on the user’s needs.

    Below is a list of all current views in the Employee Records System.

    View Purpose Access JSON Comments
    Administrative View Gives the user unfilter and unsorted access to all records in the database as well as all columns. View No
    Availability View Gives the user a department-grouped view of Active Employees to see their Last & Return Days, as well as direct access to their availability. View No This view is specifically useful for the Staff Availability System.
    All Employees Gives the user a list of all currently active employees in Cornell Retail Services. View No
    Inactive Gives the user a list of all inactive employees in Cornell Retail Services. View No Only inactive employees that have been placed inactive in the last 6 months (they get deleted afterwards).
